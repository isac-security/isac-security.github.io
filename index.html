Security and Privacy in Integrated Sensing and Communication

From Physical-Layer Threats to Experimental Validation

Abstract

As 6G networks move toward standardization, Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC) becomes a central functionality. This integration introduces new attack surfaces and raises significant privacy concerns, as wireless systems can directly sense and infer information about their surroundings.

This tutorial covers threat modeling, formal performance limits, and experimental results from practical testbeds. Topics include physical-layer attacks (jamming, spoofing, ghost-target injection), cooperative mitigation strategies, identity disclosure in cellular systems, CSI-based activity and location inference, and privacy-preserving countermeasures.
